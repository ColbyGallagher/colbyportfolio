About me

I began my journey in engineering with two years of a Bachelor's of Engineering (Aeronautical) at the University of Sydney immediately after leaving Year 12. This proved to be a poor decision as it was clearly not the field I was truly passionate for. At the end of my second year I decided on a change and found an Apprentice Civil/Structural Draftsman position available at Lindsay Dynan. This was the moment of realisation that this was the industry I wanted to spend my life in. Upon completing my apprenticeship, and undertaking a season of cricket in the UK, I returned with the determination to get a Degree in Civil Engineering.

Combining my studies in engineering with business has opened up a new world of possibilities for me. I now see and understand the financial side of engineering and how it fits into the national and international economic landscape. My studies in innovation have also led me to pursue Android App development (Java) and inclusion in the Slingshot Pre-Accelerator Startup Program.

I'm a huge supporter of embracing new technologies such as BIM, AR and AI and I firmly believe these will have a massive influence on our industry in the next 10 years. For young engineers this means having the skills necessary to succeed in a rapidly changing world, whether they be in programming, visualisation or management of international sub consultants.

During my time at University I have also run a Kickstarter campaign (unsuccessfully) for the worlds smallest portable phone charger; created and was Vice President of the Newcastle University Fraternity and I've created a Facebook page with around 3000 followers that notifies students of free food around campus.

Most recently I have been creating AR and VR applications for Android and iOS to help people sell easier. Whether it's houses, food, clothes or cars, VR and AR are a way to help people make more sales.
